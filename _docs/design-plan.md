## Problem Statement

Geodocs.dev is being positioned as the definitive GEO/AEO knowledge hub for SEO professionals, developers, and AI agents.

Strategic context:
- GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) and AEO (AI Engine Optimization) are the next evolution of SEO.
- AI search behavior is now mainstream (ChatGPT scale, Google AI Overviews at search scale), but practical GEO/AEO knowledge remains fragmented.
- Existing resources are scattered across marketing blogs and paid funnels, with limited machine-readable structure for AI-agent citation.

Execution reality:
- The site already runs on a Docusaurus fork and is live.
- We need a markdown-first content system that is equally readable by humans and machines.
- We need to scale content architecture to long-term reference depth without a full frontend rebuild in v1.

## Demand Evidence

From this office-hours session:
- User signal: people are already visiting, reading, and discussing GEO content because the topic is changing quickly.
- User belief: if geodocs disappears, replacement quality is poor.
- Practical behavior target: SEO strategists need fast-reference material for proposals and execution.

## Status Quo

Current user workaround:
- Search via Google + AI engines.
- Read fragmented sources.
- Spend significant time synthesizing (estimated 10-20 hours/week for newer practitioners).
- Low trust due to inconsistency and lack of tactical specificity.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Content-First Overlay
Summary: Keep all existing blocks exactly as-is and only swap copy.
Effort: S
Risk: Low
Pros:
- Fastest to ship.
- Low engineering risk.
Cons:
- ICP mismatch persists in several section labels.
- Weak strategic signal.
Reuses:
- 100% current homepage components.

### Approach B: Semantics Reframe (Chosen)
Summary: Keep structural layout, but reframe section meaning, headings, CTA flow, and content taxonomy around SEO strategist workflow.
Effort: M-L
Risk: Med
Pros:
- Fast enough to ship in short cycles.
- Fixes ICP mismatch without full redesign.
- Reuses existing code and blocks.
Cons:
- Requires careful editorial discipline.
- Some legacy naming may need phased cleanup.
Reuses:
- Existing homepage component skeleton and most routes.

### Approach C: ICP-First Full Homepage Rewrite
Summary: Redesign homepage IA and component composition from scratch around buyer journey.
Effort: L
Risk: Med
Pros:
- Strongest long-term architecture.
- Maximum clarity for new visitors.
Cons:
- Slowest path.
- High chance of overbuilding before message validation.
Reuses:
- Partial only.

## Recommended Approach

Choose Approach B.

Why:
- It captures most strategic upside with controlled execution risk.
- It honors current project momentum (already live and deployable).
- It lets us iterate messaging with real traffic sooner.
